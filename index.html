I'm an engineer by education but storyteller by passion. So here's a story, hope you like it

Like every child I was fascinated by stories, they were always magical. Somewhere as I grew up I lost touch with this world, I imagine it was due to the disillusionment reality brings. Life this point onwards became all about logic and reasoning.

Engineering (2015-2019)

I found deep interest towards how things worked, because all things had to have a reason, right? The question "Why?" became a very frequent thing. I got into the field of engineering to find my answers. Funnily, I got more interested in the idea of Marketing than engineering. I did not know why, but good marketing spoke to something within me.

Towards the end of college I realised I hadn't done justice for my curiosity of technology. I had done three internships, all of which taught me more about the corporate world and dealing with people from different walks of life, than technology.

Research (2019-2020)

I got the opportunity of working at the Indian Institute of Technology (Delhi), this college had been a dream of mine ever since I wanted to be an engineer. The project was on the Fourth Industrial Revolution, a domain I was well acquainted with due to my internships and college courses.

I have been working there for over 1.5 years deeply involved in research. I developed a strong intuition and understanding for technology, I learnt that I was quick at grasping the crux of it, even if the technology was new to me. At IIT I satisfied the craving to test my mettle in technology.

Struggle

I was lost and I applied for higher studies. I didn't know what I wanted to do next, and it made logical sense that I pursue my research interest as a Master's student. I spent quite a few months on preparing my application and sending it to various colleges. I was all set to pursue my Master's in Robotics from either one of my admits from USA and Germany. But a voice inside kept trying to fight its way out, it had been there ever since I stopped believing in stories, just waiting for me to stop running and introspect.

The pandemic came as a blessing to me. It was then when I re-kindled the spark, the child, the storyteller in me. I sat and watched the best TED talks of all time at a stretch and consolidated my learnings from those. I was in awe at the effect stories had on me, and I understood the importance of stories in my life. The talks helped me form a better idea of the impact I wanted to create and I felt better equipped to decide what I wanted to do with my life.

Figuring it out

In the past I had worked in two major domains, Corporate and Research. Why I wasn't fit to work in any of these helped me determine what I felt passionate about.

Corporate

Corporate life has its perks, but I would never be able to give my 100% working for someone else knowing that I was replaceable. Additionally it would separate me from technology and I want to shape the direction our society develops technology towards.

Research

I love building technology, but I can not see myself not playing a role into getting the technology in the hands of consumers. As much I love working on technology, I love building products that people love more.

Answer

I wanted to be actively involved in all verticals of a venture. I thought hard for long, trying to figure out the right space for myself.

1. I understand what things have market demand, how to create demand and how to sell something.

2. I have a strong intuition and clear visualisation of technology with which I can break down any problem into clear and easy to understand steps.

3. I can easily communicate and get my point across to various teams, helping me deliver the solution in a lucid manner.

Putting it together, I believe I can be the interface between technology and customers to drive the collaborative effort of a team towards building beautiful products which show people a better future.
